# Doripe B2B Blog SEO Strategy Design

Date: 2026-05-23
Status: Approved direction, pending user review

## 1. Goal

Doripe will build a B2B blog as a long-term SEO asset for local space operators.

The blog is not primarily a short-term sales landing page, investor memo, or product announcement feed. Its first job is to capture search demand from owners and operators of small local spaces who are trying to get more qualified visitors.

Primary success criterion:

- Long-term organic search traffic from local space marketing queries.

Secondary outcomes:

- Build trust with potential pilot stores before outreach.
- Give Doripe a credible point of view on local discovery.
- Create reusable assets for future B2B sales, pilot reports, and demo-day storytelling.

## 2. Audience

Primary audience:

- Owners and operators of small local spaces.

This includes:

- Restaurants and small dining spaces.
- Cafes and dessert shops.
- Bars, wine bars, tea houses, and quiet night spaces.
- Lifestyle shops, select shops, bookstores, flower shops, and studios.
- Galleries, pop-ups, workshops, and small exhibition spaces.

The blog should avoid narrowing the brand to only cafes. "Cafe marketing" can appear as an article-level keyword, but the category-level positioning is "local space marketing" or "small space marketing."

## 3. Positioning

Recommended blog positioning:

> A local marketing playbook for small spaces.

Doripe's point of view:

- Small spaces do not only need more exposure.
- They need to become memorable, save-worthy, and visit-worthy for the right people.
- Search, maps, reviews, Instagram, photos, atmosphere, and visit context all work together.
- Doripe's eventual B2B product can help by turning spaces into taste-based cards and testing whether users save or choose them.

The blog should not sound like an SEO agency promising shortcuts or "top ranking." It should sound like a practical operator guide written by a team that understands how people choose places.

## 4. Reference Patterns

Slack reference links from `#04-cmo-b2b`:

- Channel Talk Blog: https://channel.io/ko/blog
- ZUZU Blog: https://zuzu.network/resource/blog/

What to borrow from Channel Talk:

- Clear resource hub structure.
- Category navigation.
- Mix of insights, use cases, tips, cases, and downloadable-style resources.
- Product CTA present but not always the main character.

What to borrow from ZUZU:

- Evergreen, search-friendly practical content.
- Topic/type segmentation.
- Playbooks and guides that help the reader make a decision.
- A library feeling rather than a campaign page feeling.

Recommended Doripe adaptation:

- Use Channel Talk's resource hub clarity.
- Use ZUZU's evergreen practical depth.
- Keep Doripe product exposure light in top-of-funnel SEO articles.

## 6. Product Exposure Rule

The chosen exposure level is light.

Article body:

- 80-90% practical marketing guidance.
- 10-20% Doripe point of view or soft CTA.

Do:

- Mention Doripe as a lens or optional next step.
- Use phrasing such as "save-worthy," "visit candidate," "taste-matched discovery," and "space card."
- Add a soft CTA at the end of relevant articles.

Avoid:

- Turning every article into a Doripe product pitch.
- Overclaiming user volume, ROI, or proven store results before evidence exists.
- Promising Naver Place top ranking or traffic manipulation.

Example soft CTA:

> Doripe is preparing a pilot that introduces small spaces through taste-based cards. If you want to see how your space could be framed as a save-worthy visit candidate, apply for the pilot.

## 7. Article Template

Each SEO article should follow a repeatable structure:

1. Start from the searcher's problem.
2. Explain why the problem happens.
3. Give a practical checklist.
4. Include examples across restaurants, cafes, shops, bars, and other spaces when relevant.
5. List common mistakes.
6. Add a short Doripe perspective.
7. End with a light CTA to the pilot or space-card test.

Template constraints:

- The first screen should answer the query quickly.
- Use specific examples instead of abstract marketing language.
- Avoid keyword stuffing.
- Avoid thin near-duplicate posts when scaling to many articles.
- Keep claims evidence-based.

## 8. SEO Strategy

Primary search intent:

- Operators searching for ways to bring in more customers, improve discovery, and make their space more appealing online.

Core keyword families:

- Small store marketing.
- Local marketing.
- Restaurant promotion.
- Cafe promotion.
- Shop promotion.
- Naver Place management.
- Instagram marketing for stores.
- Short-form ideas for spaces.
- Store photo ideas.
- Review and save behavior.
- Space branding.

Recommended first cluster:

- Problem-led articles, not industry-led articles.

Reason:

- Restaurants, cafes, shops, bars, and studios share many marketing pains.
- Problem-led content lets Doripe avoid being perceived as a cafe-only brand.
- It also creates stronger internal linking because each problem can link to examples across multiple space types.

## 10. Information Architecture

Minimum blog hub requirements:

- Blog index page with category filters.
- Article list with title, summary, category, and publish date.
- Article detail page.
- Related articles by problem cluster.
- Light CTA module at article end.

Future nice-to-haves:

- Tag filters by space type: restaurant, cafe, shop, bar, studio, exhibition.
- Downloadable checklist articles.
- Pilot report examples.
- Case study collection once real pilots exist.

SEO metrics:

- Search impressions.
- Query growth by cluster.
- Click-through rate from search.
- Average position for target keyword families.

Engagement metrics:

- Article scroll depth.
- Related article clicks.
- CTA clicks.
- Business pilot page visits from blog.
- Business lead submissions from blog.

Editorial quality metrics:

- Number of non-duplicate, useful articles published.
- Coverage across problem clusters.
- Coverage across space types.
- Internal links per article.

## 12. Risks

Risk: The blog becomes cafe-only.

Mitigation:

- Use "small spaces" or "local spaces" as the category-level language.
- Rotate examples across restaurants, cafes, shops, bars, and cultural spaces.

Risk: Large-scale generation produces thin content.

Mitigation:

- Use cluster-specific outlines.
- Require concrete examples and checklists in every article.
- Review the first 20 articles manually before scaling.

Risk: Product CTA weakens SEO trust.

Mitigation:

- Keep product exposure light.
- Only mention Doripe where the article's topic naturally connects to save-worthy discovery or space cards.

Risk: Competing directly with marketing agencies on "top ranking" keywords.

Mitigation:

- Do not lead with ranking hacks.
- Lead with practical discovery, visitor intent, atmosphere, content quality, and save behavior.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

The strategy is ready for implementation when:

- The blog positioning is "local marketing playbook for small spaces."
- The initial content system is problem-led, not cafe-only or Doripe-only.
- The first 100 articles can be mapped to the 8 clusters.
- The article template keeps Doripe exposure light.
- The blog IA supports category browsing, article pages, related articles, and CTA.
- Measurement includes both SEO traffic and eventual B2B conversion from blog.

## 14. Open Decisions For Implementation Plan
